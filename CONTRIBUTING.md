Intuex Contributing Guidelines

Thank you for your interest in contributing to an Intuex project!
We welcome contributions from individuals and organizations who share our commitment to open, ethical, and non-commercial software development.

By participating in this project, you agree to follow these guidelines and the terms of the Intuex Open Source License v0.1 (Non-Commercial).

Code of Conduct

All contributors must:

Be respectful and professional

Avoid harassment, discrimination, or hostile behavior

Engage in constructive and good-faith discussion

Respect differing viewpoints and experiences

Intuex reserves the right to remove contributions or contributors that violate these principles.

Who Can Contribute

You may contribute if:

You are an individual contributor

You are contributing on behalf of an organization with permission

You agree to license your contribution under the Intuex Open Source License v0.1

Your contribution is non-commercial in intent

Contributions from entities attempting to bypass the non-commercial restrictions will be rejected.

Types of Contributions

We welcome:

Bug fixes

Feature improvements

Performance optimizations

Documentation improvements

Tests and benchmarks

Accessibility and usability enhancements

Examples and tutorials

We may decline contributions that:

Introduce commercial tracking, monetization, or licensing hooks

Add proprietary dependencies

Conflict with the project’s mission or license

Reduce clarity, security, or maintainability

Contribution License Agreement

By submitting a contribution, you agree that:

Your contribution is licensed under the Intuex Open Source License v0.1

Intuex Organization may redistribute your contribution as part of the Software

Intuex Organization may relicense your contribution under future versions of the Intuex License that preserve:

Attribution

Openness

Non-commercial protections

This agreement is automatic and does not require a separate signed document.

Original Work Requirement

You certify that:

Your contribution is your original work or

You have the legal right to submit it under this license

Do not submit:

Code copied from proprietary or incompatible licenses

Code subject to NDAs or employer restrictions

AI-generated content that violates third-party licenses

Plagiarism or misrepresentation will result in permanent removal of contribution rights.

Non-Commercial Policy

All contributions must comply with the non-commercial nature of the project.

You may not:

Add features intended primarily for commercial exploitation

Introduce paid-service integrations without approval

Use contributions as a backdoor to proprietary products

If you wish to build commercial features:

Contact Intuex Organization to discuss a commercial licensing arrangement

How to Contribute

Fork the repository

Create a new branch for your change

git checkout -b feature/your-feature-name


Make your changes

Test thoroughly

Commit with clear messages

git commit -m "Add feature: concise description"


Open a Pull Request (PR) with:

Clear description of changes

Reasoning and context

Any relevant issue references

Coding Standards

Unless otherwise specified:

Follow existing project style and conventions

Write clear, readable, and maintainable code

Avoid unnecessary complexity

Prefer clarity over cleverness

Include comments where intent is not obvious

Code that does not meet quality standards may be requested for revision.

Documentation Standards

If your change affects users:

Update relevant documentation

Include examples where helpful

Keep language clear and concise

Documentation contributions are as valuable as code contributions.

Attribution & Credit

Contributors will be credited via:

Git commit history

Release notes (when applicable)

Contributor listings (when maintained)

Do not remove existing copyright headers or attribution notices.

Review Process

All contributions are reviewed by Intuex maintainers

Feedback may be requested before acceptance

Reviews focus on:

Code quality

License compliance

Security

Maintainability

Alignment with project goals

Intuex Organization reserves the right to accept, modify, or reject any contribution.

Legal & Enforcement

Contributions that violate the license or guidelines may be removed

Abuse of the contribution process may result in bans

Legal action may be taken in cases of intentional license abuse

All contributions are governed by the Intuex Open Source License v0.1.
